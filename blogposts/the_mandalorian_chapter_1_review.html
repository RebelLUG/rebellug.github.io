The Mandalorian: Chapter 1 - RebelLUG Review

What did the RebelLUG members think of the first chapter of the Mandalorian?

11/15/19

Jon Favreau’s The Mandalorian stars Pedro Pascal, and this episode is directed by the one and only Dave Filoni. The series aired earlier this week, so let’s talk about Chapter One.

The Mandalorian picks off after Return of the Jedi and before The Force Awakens. But I will say that the series has a look much more similar to the original trilogy rather than the sequels.

Another important note is the CGI. It’s not used too heavily, but when it’s used it’s obvious. While I much prefer practical effects in Star Wars, I understand it’s use to keep costs down.

However RebelLUG member brickninja mentioned that the show has a 12 million dollar budget per episode. And with that in consideration, I can’t understand why the CGI looks slightly unfinished.

Member legospencer brought up that it felt like there was “Lots of cliche / overly trying to be badass dialogue.” I would generally agree. However, the badass dialogue could be there to show the audience how the arrogant and hotheaded mentality of the mandalorian. I’m sure this will be explored more later in the series, and I’m interested to see if his tone will change.

Moreover, Pedro Pascal does a fantastic job portraying The Mandalorian. His spoken lines are minimal, which adds to the mystery of the character. Most of his emotions and intentions are conveyed through hand gestures and subtle head tilts.

IG-11, voiced by Taika Watiki is hilarious. There’s a joke referenced frequently that made me smirk every single time. Perfect casting.

The Ending also is a perfect cliffhanger, just giving the audience a quick taste of Chapter Two.

As far as a rating goes, RebelLUG members gave this episode an 7 out of 10 average. Regardless, it’s a promising series so I would check it out on Disney Plus.
